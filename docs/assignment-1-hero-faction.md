## What Is a Hero/Faction Screen?

A hero screen is an interactive single-page experience where the user chooses between 3–5 options (characters, factions, collections, etc.) and the **entire page shifts** to reflect each option on hover — colors, typography, mood, atmosphere.

**Not a gamer?** Think of it as an interactive poster. A band page, movie landing page, or fashion lookbook where hovering between options transforms the whole composition.

The word "hero" in design = the dominant visual element. A hero screen is a page where that dominant element changes based on interaction.

## Your Role: Art Director + Orchestrator

You are the **Art Director**. AI is your engineering team. You do not write the CSS by hand — you direct the AI, then evaluate whether the result matches your vision.

> "The Art Director never says 'make it look cool.' They say 'the palette is desaturated teal with warm amber accents, the type is condensed sans-serif at 72pt for headers, and when the user hovers, the background shifts from 15% to 85% opacity over 300ms.' That level of specificity is what makes the AI useful. Without it, you get generic."
> — Professor Lindsey

## ESF Practices (Epistemic Stewardship Framework)

### 1. Design Intent
A creative spec written **before AI touches any code**. Defines:
- Specific color values (e.g. `#1a1a2e` background, `#d4a843` accent — not "dark and moody")
- Type choices with sizes
- Hover behavior described precisely
- Mood in one sentence
- Non-negotiables

**Must be human-authored. AI-generated Design Intents = academic dishonesty.**

### 2. AI Direction Log (3–5 entries)
Each entry captures:
1. What you asked AI to do
2. What it produced
3. What you changed, rejected, or kept — and **why**

Lives in: README, annotated git commit messages, or sketchbook.

### 3. Records of Resistance (3 moments)
Three documented moments where you rejected or significantly revised AI output. Each answers:
- What did AI produce?
- Why did you reject or revise it?
- What did you do instead?

### 4. Five Questions Reflection
Answer before submitting:
1. **Can I defend this?** Can I explain every major decision?
2. **Is this mine?** Does it reflect my creative direction, or did I follow AI's suggestions?
3. **Did I verify?** Did I check that things work the way I think they work?
4. **Would I teach this?** Do I understand it well enough to explain to someone else?
5. **Is my documentation honest?** Does my AI Direction Log accurately describe what I asked and what I changed?

## Tips for Success

- **Start with Design Intent, not code.** Going straight to AI = generic output.
- **Sketch on paper first.** Draw the layout. Label sections. Annotate hover behavior.
- **Use the First Playable (Session 4).** Screen must be live on GitHub Pages even if rough.
- **Scope down.** 3–5 factions. One page. No routing. No backend.
- **Test in incognito.** Open GitHub Pages URL in incognito before submitting. If it doesn't load, the submission is broken.

## Academic Integrity

- Individual assignment — no shared code, Design Intents, or documentation
- Design Intent must be your own writing (written before AI engagement)
- AI Direction Log and Records of Resistance must be honest
- Fabricated documentation is worse than missing documentation
